Who is he?

Isaac Newton, born on January 4, 1643, in Woolsthorpe, England, was a renowned mathematician, physicist, and astronomer. His groundbreaking work laid the foundation for classical mechanics and the laws of motion. Newton's Early Years

Before The Birth of Newton

Isaac Newton's father, also named Isaac Newton, died three months before Newton was born on January 4, 1643. The elder Newton was a prosperous farmer, but he passed away at the age of 36, leaving his widow, Hannah Ayscough Newton, to raise their son in Woolsthorpe, Lincolnshire, England.

The Birth of Newton

Isaac Newton was born on January 4, 1643, according to the Julian calendar in use in England at that time. However, when the Gregorian calendar was adopted later, the date became January 4, 1643, in the Julian calendar and January 15, 1643, in the Gregorian calendar, which is the calendar system most widely used today.

Newton's Early Life

Isaac Newton's mother, Hannah Ayscough Newton, remarried when Isaac was three years old. After her second marriage to Barnabas Smith, the family moved to nearby North Witham, leaving Isaac in the care of his maternal grandmother for a few years until his mother returned a widow after her second husband's death.

Newton's School Life

Newton attended Trinity College, Cambridge, and his early education was interrupted by the outbreak of the bubonic plague in 1665. During this time, known as the "Plague Year," the university was closed, and Newton returned to his hometown.
